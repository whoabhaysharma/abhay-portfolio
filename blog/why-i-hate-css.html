Why I Hate CSS (And Why I Still Use It)

// DATE: 2026-01-15
// MOOD: ANNOYED
// TAGS: #CSS #FRONTEND

CSS is like a relationship where you give 100% and it gives you a slightly misaligned div in return.

Everything feels fine until you open your perfectly crafted layout on Safari, and suddenly your footer is floating in the stratosphere.

The Center Div Incident

I tried to center a button today. It took 4 hours.

"CSS is easy," they said. "It's just styling," they said.

I wrote this:

.container {
    display: flex;
    justify-content: center; /* Hope */
    align-items: center;     /* Prayer */
}

And it worked. But then I added margin-top to a child element and the entire layout collapsed like a house of cards in a hurricane. Why? Because of margin collapse. A feature designed by someone who clearly hated humanity.

Z-Index Wars

I have a modal. I give it z-index: 9999. It stays behind the header. I give the header z-index: 10. The modal is still behind. I eventually set z-index: 2147483647 because I refuse to negotiate with terrorists.

Why Do We Do This?

  • Because we like pain?
  • Because Tailwind exists but we are purists?
  • Because deep down, when it finally aligns, we feel like gods.
